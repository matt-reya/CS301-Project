# CS 301 Project
###### Makuta Allen (mma253@njit.edu), John Burghardt (jab324@njit.edu), Longa Silungwe (ls568@njit.edu), Matthew Weir (mrw34@njit.edu)
This project is aimed to uncover how traffic volume affects air quality in New York City. By analyzing and combining traffic volume data and air quality data, we can determine how higher traffic volumes worsens the air quality in New York City. 
## Data sources:
Traffic volume: https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Transportation/Traffic-Volume-Counts-Historical-/btm5-ppia/about_data  
Air quality: https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Environment/Air-Quality-and-Health-Impacts/c3uy-2p5r
## Initial hypothesis:
H<sub>0</sub>: Traffic volume doesn't have an effect on the air quality in New York City.  
H<sub>A</sub>: Higher traffic volume worsens the air quality(air pollution) in New York City.
